## Summary
Passive re-reading is ineffective. The Cornell method and active recall force your brain to retrieve information rather than just recognize it, which is what produces durable memory.

## Main Content

### Cornell Note-Taking Method

Divide the page into three sections:

```
┌─────────────────┬──────────────────────────────┐
│   Cue Column    │        Notes Column           │
│   (keywords,    │   (main content, taken        │
│   questions)    │    during class/reading)      │
│                 │                               │
├─────────────────┴──────────────────────────────┤
│                  Summary                        │
│          (written after, in own words)          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Process:**
1. **Take notes** in the right column during class
2. **Write cues** in the left column after class (keywords, questions that the notes answer)
3. **Write summary** at bottom in your own words
4. **Review** by covering notes, using cue column to prompt recall

### Active Recall
- Retrieve information from memory rather than re-reading
- Methods: flashcards, practice problems, teaching someone else, closing notes and writing what you remember
- Spacing effect: review at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks)

### Effective Study Session Structure
1. **Before:** Write down what you already know about the topic
2. **During:** Take Cornell notes, mark confusion with `?`
3. **After (same day):** Fill cue column, write summary
4. **Next day:** Active recall using cue column only
5. **End of week:** Review summaries only

### What Doesn't Work
- Highlighting without engaging
- Re-reading passively
- Cramming (no spacing)
- Studying in noisy, distraction-heavy environments

## Key Insights
- The act of retrieval itself strengthens memory — testing is not just assessment, it's learning
- Writing summaries in your own words forces comprehension, not just recognition
- Confusion markers (`?`) during notes prevent the illusion of understanding
- Spaced repetition compounds — 5 short sessions beat 1 long session every time
